Manual Pallet Stacker

A full 3D model and photorealistic render set of a manual hydraulic pallet stacker, built for a client who needed clean visual assets before the physical unit was produced. My role was strictly modeling and rendering: the machine's engineering and design were provided as a brief, and the deliverable was a set of believable, production-ready visuals.

The set includes a human operator for scale and context, showing the stacker mid-use — mast raised, cables engaged, forks positioned under a wooden pallet — across close-up, working, and profile shots.

TaskModeling + rendering only
FocusMechanical detail, human scale
Views03 angles
OutputPhotoreal stills

02 / 05

Leather Bucket Bag

Product visualization for a leather bucket bag with a drawstring closure and brass hardware, rendered for catalog-style presentation. The goal was to sell the material — soft, structured leather with visible weight and drape — under studio lighting that feels close to a real product photoshoot.

Front and back turnarounds were produced so the client could review stitching, hardware placement, and the strap construction from every practical angle.

TaskModeling + material + rendering
MaterialStudio-lit leather shader
ViewsFront, front alt, back

Bag-Shaped Boutique

A concept retail pavilion shaped like an oversized handbag — an experiential-retail idea where the architecture itself is the branding. The leather-like shell folds around a display arch and checkout counter, with the handle doubling as the store's entrance frame.

Rendered from exterior and interior-facing angles to communicate scale, structure, and how a shopper would actually move through the space.

TaskConcept modeling + rendering
TypeExperiential retail pavilion
ViewsExterior, interior detail, structural back

04 / 05

Lion Character Keychain

A soft, plush-style lion head keychain modeled in two production variants: a rounded Standard volume for full 3D-printed or molded keychains, and a low-relief Flat version sized for acrylic or enamel pin production. Building both from a single base sculpt kept the character consistent across product formats.

The renders below show the Standard rounded variant from front, three-quarter, and profile angles, used to check silhouette, fur-strand sculpting, and shading before either variant went to production.

TaskCharacter modeling + rendering
VariantsStandard (rounded) · Flat
ViewsFront, 3/4, profile

SketchUp Furniture Models

A set of furniture pieces modeled to spec in SketchUp for a client's catalog: a sculptural, CNC-cut wave bench built from stacked plywood ribs, a cane-back armchair with a curved wooden frame and woven rattan panel, and a matching cane chaise lounge for the same collection. Each piece was modeled for accurate proportions and construction logic, then rendered to preview finish and form.

Roblox Wing Accessory

A stylized wing/blade accessory designed for Roblox, built around a playing-card theme: a gold-trimmed blade split into checkered, ace-red, and card-suit panels, finished with a cracked, shattered-glass surface texture and a row of bullet-shaped gold studs along the spine.

Modeled as a low-poly-friendly asset with a bold silhouette that reads clearly at small in-game scale, then rendered both as a pair and as a single blade to check profile and texture detail.

PlatformRoblox accessory
TaskModeling + texturing + rendering
ThemePlaying cards / shattered glass

Gripy Sandals — Size Range

Product visualization for "Gripy," a webbed, open-cage slide sandal built around an organic, root-like support structure. Modeled on a foot mesh to check fit and coverage, then rendered across the size run so the client could confirm proportions scale correctly from smaller to larger fits.

Two sizes are shown here in different colorways — a lighter size 5 fit and a size 7 fit in a deeper tone — each rendered from a front and side angle to check strap placement and branding legibility.
